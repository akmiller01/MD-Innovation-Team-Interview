Most Marylanders work in the service-providing sector. Jobs cover a wide spectrum: from government positions to transportation-related professions, from wholesale trade to the finance and insurance industry. In 2023, 19% of the workforce was employed by federal, State and local governments, while 81% of workers were employed in the private sector.


Mural, East Antietam St. (near North Potomac St.), Hagerstown, Maryland, June 2004. Photo by Diane F. Evartt.

Maryland's workforce is among the best educated in the nation. In 2022, over 43.8% of its population aged 25 or older held a bachelor's degree or higher, including 20.6% who held a graduate or professional degree.

In 2021, Maryland's workforce ranked second in the country for its high percentage of professional and technical workers (30.2%). Moreover, in 2021, Maryland had the fifth highest concentration of doctoral scientists and engineers in the nation. Also in 2021, Maryland had the second highest concentration of science, technology, engineering, & math (STEM) workers nationally, with 258,230 employees. Maryland, in 2020, was fourth in the nation for employment within the federal government, though it ranked first for federal jobs per capita, at 242 jobs per 10,000 residents.
